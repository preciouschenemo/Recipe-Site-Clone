Spaghetti Omelette

This recipe transforms leftover spaghetti into a delightful and satisfying frittata-style dish. It's a great way to use up leftovers and create a delicious and easy meal.

prep time

10 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

3 servings

Tags : Main courses Breakfast Snacks and Desserts Sides

instructions

step 1

If you don't have leftover spaghetti, cook it according to package directions in a large pot of boiling salted water. Drain the spaghetti well and set aside.

step 2

In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, grated cheese, salt, and pepper. Add any desired chopped vegetables or cooked meat to the egg mixture and stir to combine.

step 3

Heat the olive oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, swirl it to coat the bottom of the pan. Pour in half of the egg mixture and tilt the pan to spread the mixture evenly. Add half of the cooked spaghetti to one half of the omelette.

step 4

Once the edges of the omelette start to set and the bottom is cooked (around 5 minutes), use a spatula to fold the other half of the omelette over the spaghetti filling. Gently press down on the omelette with the spatula to ensure it cooks evenly. Cook for another minute or two, or until the omelette is cooked through and the cheese is melted. Slide the omelette onto a serving plate and enjoy!

ingredients

2 cups cooked spaghetti

3 large eggs

1/4 cup milk

1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese (or any preferred cheese)

1 tablespoon olive oil

Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Optional additions: Chopped cooked vegetables (broccoli, peas, etc.), chopped cooked meat (ham, sausage, etc.)

tools

Large pot

Colander

Large bowl

Whisk

Non-stick pan

Spatula

Serving plates
