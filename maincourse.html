Ndole

This recipe captures the essence of Ndole, a beloved Cameroonian dish featuring a vibrant peanut soup base simmered with bitterleaf, meat, and shrimp.

prep time

45 min.

cook time

60 min.

serving

4-6 servings

Tags : Main courses Breakfast Snacks and Desserts Sides

instructions

step 1 (Prepare the base)

In a dry pan over medium heat, toast the peanuts for 5-7 minutes, stirring frequently, until fragrant and lightly browned. Remove from heat and let cool slightly. In a blender or food processor, combine the toasted peanuts, chopped onion, garlic, ginger, hot water, and broth. Blend until smooth and creamy. Transfer the peanut mixture to a large pot or Dutch oven. Stir in the crayfish powder (or blended crayfish), tomato paste, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.

step 2 (Cook the meat)

While the peanut base simmers, heat palm oil (or vegetable oil) in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add the chicken or beef and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until browned on all sides. If using shrimp, add them to the pan with the cooked meat and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until pink and opaque. Remove the cooked meat and shrimp from the pan and set aside.

step 3 (Simmer the stew)

Add the chopped ndolè leaves, scotch bonnet pepper (adjust for desired spice level), and bay leaf to the simmering peanut base. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the ndolè leaves are tender. If the soup thickens too much, add a little more broth or water to thin it out.

step 4 (Finish the ndole)

Return the cooked meat and shrimp (if using) to the pot with the simmering ndolè soup. Stir gently to combine and heat through for a few minutes. Season with additional salt and black pepper to taste.

step 5 (Serve and Enjoy)

Ladle the Ndole soup into bowls and serve hot with your favorite sides like fufu, plantains, or rice.

ingredients

1 cup raw peanuts (skinless)

1 medium onion, chopped

4 garlic cloves, minced

1 inch ginger, grated

1 cup chicken broth or vegetable broth

1/2 cup crayfish powder (or 1 cup whole crayfish, soaked and blended)

1 tablespoon tomato paste

1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika

Salt and black pepper to taste

1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs or beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces

1/2 pound fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined (optional)

pound ndolè leaves (fresh or frozen), washed and chopped (soak fresh leaves for 30 minutes if using)

1 tablespoon palm oil (or vegetable oil)

1 scotch bonnet pepper (deseeded and chopped, adjust for desired spice level)

1 bay leaf

Optional vegetables (chopped): 1 cup green bell pepper, 1/2 cup chopped spinach

tools

Blender or food processor

Large Pot With Lid

Medium saucepan

Knife

Cutting board

Spoon

Serving ladle

Eru

This recipe features Eru leaves (angled melon leaves), a unique ingredient that lends a distinct flavor to this flavorful Cameroonian soup.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

30-45 min.

serving

4-6 servings

Tags : Main courses Breakfast Snacks and Desserts Sides

instructions

step 1

Heat palm oil (or vegetable oil) in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Stir in the ground crayfish, thyme, scotch bonnet pepper, salt, and black pepper.

step 2

Add the chicken or beef to the pot and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until browned on all sides. If using smoked fish, add it to the pot with the cooked meat and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.

step 3

Add the chopped spinach or waterleaves to the pot and cook for 2-3 minutes, until wilted. Drain the soaked eru leaves and add them to the pot with the broth. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the eru leaves are softened and tender. If using crayfish powder, stir it into the soup during the last few minutes of simmering. If using whole crayfish, add them with the broth and simmer for the entire time. Add additional broth or water as needed to achieve your desired soup consistency.

step 4 (Serve and enjoy)

Ladle the Eru soup into bowls and serve hot with your favorite sides like fufu, plantains, or rice.
Tips: Soaking the eru leaves helps to soften them and reduce any bitterness. However, some people prefer a slightly bitter taste. Adjust the soaking time according to your preference. Adjust the amount of scotch bonnet pepper according to your desired spice level. Eru soup can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently over low heat before serving. Enjoy this flavorful and unique dish from Cameroon!

ingredients

1/2 cup palm oil

1 scotch bonnet pepper (deseeded and chopped, adjust for desired spice level)

1 teaspoon ground crayfish

1/2 tablespoon salt

1/4 teaspoon black pepper

1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs or beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces

1 cup chopped spinach or waterleaves

8 cups dried eru leaves (soaked in warm water for 30 minutes)

1 cup chopped smoked fish (optional)

1 cup crayfish powder (or 1 cup whole crayfish, soaked and blended)

4 cups chicken broth or vegetable broth (additional broth may be needed)

tools

Medium saucepan

Large Heavy Pot With Lid

Spoon

Cutting board

Knife

Serving ladle

Roast Fish

This recipe features a whole fish simply seasoned with herbs and lemon, roasted to perfection for a delicious and healthy Cameroonian-inspired dish.

prep time

15 min.

cook time

20 min.

serving

3 servings

Tags : Main courses Breakfast Snacks and Desserts Sides

instructions

step 1

Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C)

step 2

Rinse the fish thoroughly under cold running water and pat it dry with paper towels. Using a sharp knife, make 3-4 diagonal slashes on each side of the fish (optional, helps the fish cook more evenly). In a small bowl, combine olive oil, lemon juice from half of the lemon, garlic, thyme, oregano, salt, and pepper.

step 3

Rub the herb mixture all over the fish, inside the cavity, and into the slashes (if made). Stuff the cavity of the fish with the remaining lemon slices and optional fresh herbs.

step 4

Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and place the fish in a baking dish. Roast the fish in the preheated oven for 18-20 minutes, or until the flesh flakes easily with a fork and the skin is golden brown.

step 5

Carefully remove the fish from the oven using a serving spatula. Transfer the fish to a serving platter and garnish with additional lemon slices and fresh herbs (if desired). Serve the roasted fish hot with your favorite sides like roasted vegetables, rice, or plantains.

ingredients

1 whole fish (1-2 pounds), such as tilapia, snapper, or cod (cleaned and gutted)

1 tablespoon olive oil

1 lemon, sliced

2 cloves garlic, minced

1/2 teaspoon dried thyme

1/4 teaspoon dried oregano

Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

1/2 cup chopped fresh herbs (optional, such as parsley, cilantro, or basil)

tools

Baking sheet

Parchment paper

Baking dish (large enough to fit the fish comfortably)

Sharp knife

Spoon

Serving spatula

Jollof Rice

Carefully remove the fish from the oven using a serving spatula. Transfer the fish to a serving platter and garnish with additional lemon slices and fresh herbs (if desired). Serve the roasted fish hot with your favorite sides like roasted vegetables, rice, or plantains.

prep time

15 min.

cook time

35 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : Main courses Breakfast Snacks and Desserts Sides

instructions

step 1

Heat vegetable oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and translucent. Add the chopped bell pepper, garlic, ginger, and scotch bonnet pepper (adjust for desired spice level). Cook for another minute, stirring constantly, until fragrant. Stir in the chopped tomatoes (or crushed tomatoes), tomato paste, thyme, paprika, ground bay leaves, salt, and black pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, to allow the flavors to meld.

step 2

Rinse the long-grain white rice in a fine-mesh strainer until the water runs clear. Add the rinsed rice and chicken broth (or vegetable broth) to the pot with the simmering tomato base. Stir to combine and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover the pot tightly with a lid, and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked through and the liquid is absorbed. If the rice is not fully cooked after 25 minutes, add a little more broth (1/4 cup at a time) and simmer for a few more minutes until the rice is cooked.

step 3

Once the rice is cooked, remove the pot from the heat and let it stand for 5 minutes, covered, to allow the steam to distribute and fluff up the rice. Fluff the rice gently with a fork before serving.

ingredients

1/4 cup vegetable oil

1 large red onion, chopped

4 plum tomatoes, chopped (or 1 (14.5 oz) can crushed tomatoes)

1 bell pepper (red, yellow, or orange), chopped

2 garlic cloves, minced

1 inch ginger, grated

1 scotch bonnet pepper (deseeded and chopped, adjust for desired spice level)

1 teaspoon tomato paste

1 teaspoon ground thyme

1/2 teaspoon paprika

1/2 teaspoon ground bay leaves

Salt and black pepper to taste

2 cups long-grain white rice, rinsed

3 1/2 cups chicken broth or vegetable broth

1 bay leaf

tools

Large pot or Dutch oven

Knife

Cutting board

Spoon

Measuring cups
